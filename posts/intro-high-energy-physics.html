Introduction to High Energy Physics

High‑energy physics (HEP) studies nature at the smallest scales using particle accelerators and detectors. We often summarize kinematics with the energy–momentum relation:

$$E^2 = (pc)^2 + (m_0 c^2)^2$$

In natural units \(c=1\), this simplifies to \(E^2 = p^2 + m^2\).

import numpy as np
m = 0.511 # MeV (electron rest mass)
p = 1000  # MeV momentum
E = (p**2 + m**2)**0.5
print(E)
